A non-prescriptive development opportunity

The capital figures above describe a conservative three-stage pathway. They are not a price ceiling and they are not a fixed plan to fund. The Al-Qaryatayn resource can support several development models, including phased production beginning at a smaller initial commercial scale, accelerated high-volume extraction, strategic offtake-led development, or a fully integrated mine-to-market structure. Investor-led proposals at larger scale or alternative commercial structures are welcomed where technically, legally and logistically feasible.

The Invitation for Offers is the document we share once an investor identifies themselves below. It sets out what Syria Silicon brings as a developed-asset platform, the six pathways through which a partner may propose to deliver capability, the minimum elements required in any proposal, and the criteria by which proposals are evaluated.

What Syria Silicon brings

  • Verified resource: approximately 100 million tonnes Indicated equivalent in Block I (GDMR, Syrian Ministry of Petroleum and Mineral Resources). 17 samples independently analysed by ALS Arabia. Best tested sample 99.4% SiO₂.
  • Operating MoU with the Syrian Ministry of Energy, signed on the basis of mining approval.
  • In-country operating company: Nawras Alali Silica & Minerals LLC, Syrian Arab Republic.
  • Government and local relations, in-country expert team, framework for local hiring.
  • Existing site infrastructure: loading area and on-site building in place at the deposit.
  • Logistics corridor: approximately 215 km to Tartus on the Mediterranean, with parallel rail alignment available.
  • Market access: regional glass, solar glass, foundry, ferro-alloy and microsilica markets, plus domestic offtake from the Syrian reconstruction programme commissioning from 2027.

Partnership pathways

A partner may propose in one lane, or combine lanes into an integrated offer. The full proposal element list is in the Invitation for Offers.

ADevelopment capital — mine, processing, transport, water, power, working capital.
BEPC and technical delivery — design, construction, commissioning, operation.
CMining operation — fleet, contractor model, production plan, site management.
DOfftake and product strategy — long-term purchase under defined terms.
ELogistics — truck, rail, or combined logistics to port or domestic customers.
FFull integrated solution — capital, EPC, operations, logistics, processing and offtake.

How proposals are evaluated

Syria Silicon will prioritise proposals on the criteria set out in the IFO, including:

  • Speed to first production and credibility of execution plan.
  • Capacity to finance and execute without excessive conditionality.
  • Strength of offtake commitment, where applicable.
  • Technical credibility in silica mining and processing.
  • Compliance, banking and sanctions viability.
  • Long-term strategic alignment beyond a single transaction.
